In West Bengal, Rasgulla is believed to have been introduced by the famed confectioner Nobin Chandra Das in the 1860s. Das is often cr edited with refining the technique of making chhena-based sweets, which eventually led to th e creation of the spongy Rasgulla we know today.

Rasgulla recipe. Learn how to make bengali rasgulla recipe at home. Spongy Rasgulla is one of the popular Indian sweet recipes that is made by curdling milk. Then separating the chenna (paneer or indian cottage cheese) and whey by draining in a muslin cloth. The drained chenna is kneaded and then rolled to balls. These are cooked in sugar syrup till they turn light and spongy.

Rasgulla is a juicy milk based dessert loved by people all over India. These tiny juicy balls are addictive, delicious & are made during festive times. INGREDENT REQUIRED :

    For Chhena (Paneer):

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup water (to dilute lemon juice/vinegar)
  • Ice cubes or cold water (for rinsing chhena)

    • For Sugar Syrup:

  • 2 cups sugar
  • 5 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods (optional, for flavor)
  • few saffron strands (optional)
    • 1 tsp rose water (optional)

    step 1: Prepare the Chhena (Paneer)


    Heat 1 liter full-fat milk in a pan until it reaches a boil.
    Lower the heat and add 2 tbsp lemon juice (mixed with 1 cup water), stirring gently.
    When the milk curdles completely, turn off the heat. If needed, add a little more lemon juice.
    Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth or a fine sieve.
    Rinse the chhena under cold water to remove the lemon's sourness.
    Squeeze out excess water and let it rest for 30 minutes to drain completely.

    Step 2: Knead the Chhena


    Transfer the chhena to a plate and knead it for 8-10 minutes until smooth and soft.
    Divide the mixture into small equal-sized balls, rolling them smoothly without cracks.

    Step 3: Prepare the Sugar Syrup


    In a large pot, add 2 cups sugar and 5 cups water and bring to a boil.
    Add 2-3 crushed cardamom pods and rose water (optional) for flavor.

    Step 4: Cook the Rasgullas


    Gently drop the chhena balls into the boiling syrup.
    Cover the pot and boil for 15 minutes on medium heat. The rasgullas will double in size.
    After cooking, turn off the heat and let them cool in the syrup.

    Step 5: Serve


    Chill the rasgullas in the syrup for at least 4-5 hours before serving.
